ICH E6 Good Clinical Practice Guidelines - Summary

1. Informed Consent
All participants must provide written informed consent before entering a clinical trial.
The investigator is responsible for ensuring that informed consent is obtained from each subject.
Informed consent must be obtained before any trial-related procedures are performed.
The subject must be given sufficient time to consider participation in the trial.

2. Responsibilities of the Investigator
The investigator is responsible for the conduct of the trial at the trial site.
The investigator must be qualified by education, training, and experience to assume responsibility for the proper conduct of the trial.
The investigator must be thoroughly familiar with the appropriate use of the investigational product.

3. Adverse Event Reporting
All serious adverse events must be reported to the sponsor immediately.
The investigator must report serious adverse events within 24 hours of becoming aware of the event.
Follow-up reports must be submitted as additional information becomes available.

4. Record Retention
The investigator must retain trial documents for at least 15 years after completion of the trial.
The sponsor must retain all sponsor-specific essential documents for at least 15 years.
Records must be available for inspection by regulatory authorities at any time.
